About

Hi! I’m Habiba Morsy, a Ph.D. student in Data Science at the University of Virginia, advised by Professor Stephen Baek.

My research interests are in scientific machine learning, computational modeling, simulation, and data-driven approaches for physical systems. I am currently working on autonomous aerodynamic design pipelines for Formula 1 car geometries, with a focus on parametric modeling, simulation-ready datasets, and machine learning for design exploration.

Before joining UVA, I completed my B.S. in Computational Science and Engineering at Kean University, with minors in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics.

Research & Projects

Autonomous Aerodynamic Design Pipeline for Formula 1 Cars

Developing a pipeline for generating parametric Formula 1 car geometries and building simulation-ready datasets for aerodynamic analysis. This project combines CAD modeling, computational fluid dynamics, and machine learning to support data-driven design exploration.

Physics-Aware Deep Learning for Energetic Materials

Applying physics-aware deep learning models to simulate the behavior of energetic materials under extreme conditions. This work focuses on learning spatiotemporal dynamics from simulation data and evaluating model performance on detonation and non-detonation cases.

Metadata Quality Assurance using Large Language Models

Developed large language model workflows to automate quality assurance for digital library metadata. This project explored how LLMs can identify inconsistencies, missing values, and quality issues in structured academic metadata.

Programmatic Access to Structural Biology Data

Built Python tools and workflows to streamline access to structural biology data through the RCSB Protein Data Bank API, improving usability and accessibility for researchers.
